A Cursory Education On Education

JUNE 13, 2015

Towards the end of last year, I decided that I wanted to make a difference in education.

The problem was that I didn't know much more about education than my personal experiences as a student.

I knew that problems abounded, but I wanted to find the best place to make an impact, and the how to leverage that impact using the latest findings in education research.

To that end, I had a lot of questions. Luckily for me, there are people that took the time to painstakingly research, compile, analyze and write down the answers to these questions into digestible books and blogs. I present those questions and the writings that answer them below.

Why are US kids behind? What can we learn from Korea, Poland and Finland?

The Smartest Kids in the World by Amanda Ripley

What's up with grit? What non-academic skills lead to success?

How Children Succeed by Paul Tough

How do we improve teacher quality?

Building a Better Teacher by Elizabeth Green

Who is Doug Lamov and WTF is "no opt out"?

Teach Like a Champion by Doug Lemov

How does Success Academy achieve its results?

Mission Possible by Eva Moskowitz

What is KIPP? Why is it controversial? How did it get so big so fast?

Work Hard, Be Nice by Jay Mathews

What's Sal Khan vision for the future of education?

One World Schoolhouse by Sal Khan

Can we make online learning better?

Learning Online by Cristina Cacioppo
